So many innovations in software delivery have been about one thing: tightening feedback loops.

Agile shortened the cycle between plan and release. DevOps sped up the loop from code to production. Lean tightened the loop from idea to validation. XP said it plainly: if feedback is good, more feedback is better.

AI isn't different. It's just the latest loop-tightening technology.

Where the loop tightens is what changes. The underlying principles don't — fast feedback beats slow feedback.

Here's what I'm seeing play out. The teams struggling with AI coding tools are trying to bolt AI onto waterfall processes. The teams who are flying? They already thought in feedback loops. AI just makes them faster.

If your delivery process didn't work before AI, making it faster won't fix it. The constraint just moves somewhere else.
