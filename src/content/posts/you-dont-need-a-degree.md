When I got my first engineering job without a degree, I felt like I'd tricked them. But I'd worked my backside off to get to that point.

Seeing a degree as a requirement on a job ad is a massive turn off for me as it shows how narrow-minded the org is.

I've hired a lot of people with and without degrees, and I can confidently say it's not the only way. Yes, a degree gives you a lot, but so does experience and a good mentor. One is not better than the other; they're just different. A degree is a great way to get into an industry, but it doesn't guarantee it.

All the best teams I've worked in have had a mixture of self-taught and university-educated engineers. This is where the diversity of thought comes from. Not everyone can go to university, be it for economic, social or neurological reasons. Apprenticeships are a great leveller, giving people who may not suit university education the potential to thrive.
