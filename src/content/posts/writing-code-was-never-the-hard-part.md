Sure, AI can be a helpful assistant when coding, but your copilot isn't going to tell you if you're building the right thing.

More code = more overhead.

Vibe coding is focusing on the wrong thing and missing the true value of the AI revolution. Writing code is such a small part of the end-to-end process of shipping software, and there are more effective applications of AI.

- Write a good Definition of Ready and let AI validate your ticket's readiness before it hits the team.
- Write a solid peer review policy and let AI give your PR a first pass before a human takes a look.
- Lacking clarity on what versions and features are in which environment? GenAI is pretty good at summarising this for you.
- Constantly interrupted by questions? Write some FAQs and train a support bot to field them (or just do the FAQs).
- Not convinced you're building the right things? Run your product strategy through ChatGPT and ask it to challenge your thinking. It can probably help you identify measures to validate your bets.

AI hasn't changed the fundamentals, it's just widening the gap between companies that work with clarity and those that work with vibes.
