Flexible working can unlock small but significant moments.

Ever since my daughter started school, I've tried to finish early on Mondays so I can pick her up. This is easily planned around and causes no negative impact to my team, while the positives are priceless.

I remember, as a young kid, being bored after school and feeling so excited when my dad got home from work. Getting to dedicate a solid couple of hours one-on-one with my daughter and letting her decide the activity is so good for our relationship. Today, it was drawing scenes from Harry Potter.

This small adjustment works well for my workload too. Once we've gotten the kids to bed, I'll open the laptop and make up the missed time. This is one of the few prolonged periods of uninterrupted work I get, and is generally my most productive.

It's win-win.

This right here is work-life balance: finding a way to make your routine work for both your personal and professional commitments. All it takes is thinking outside of conventional norms.
